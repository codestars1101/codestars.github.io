“Whether you want to uncover the secrets of the universe, or you want to pursue a career in the 21st century, basic computer programming is an essential skill to learn.”
(Stephan Hawking)

We organized our “Hour of Code” event with the students of Sumgayit city
secondary school number 4 on the 10th of December. The major purpose of this
event is to teach them how algorithms work and why we use them.

Firstly, Abdullah talked about our event in detail and
provided children with the information about code.org. Then
Elnare talked about ADA University, especially SITE faculty, children
were listening to her carefully, then asked questions about ADA exams,
study-life. Then Leyla introduced a presentation with examples of our
daily life activities in order to help students to perceive the steps of
the algorithm. This presentation consists of an introduction of the
algorithm and types of algorithm.

After this introduction, we continued with code.org steps.
There were 20 steps in total, each of us was explaining 5 of them.
Some of the children’s intelligence surprised us. Even when we were
explaining the steps, some of them finished them on their own.
We showed Mark Zuckerberg, Bill Gates and so many people’s motivational
videos that are related to the future of coding. In the end, Togrul were
working with students to solve harder stuff and suggested to them more
courses to improve their skills in code.org.
What did the children gain?

1. They learned in more detail the essence of intelligence.
2. We were working altogether, they need to think fast, as a result, it made them more vigilant.
3. It was a motivation for children to think about programming.
4. It was a pleasure for them because they asked that will they have a chance to get a lesson again from us.

What did we gain?
1. We recognized our team-member better.
